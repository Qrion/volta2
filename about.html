    We are on a mission to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.


    The Unmet Need

    Agriculture stands for 9% of total U.S. greenhouse gas emissions. Most of which is methane, a gas 30 times more potent in trapping heat in the atmosphere than CO2. “If cattle were a country, they would rank third in greenhouse gas emissions” writes Bill Gates on Gates Notes to emphasize the importance of this issue.

    The State of California has passed a bill stating that farmers must reduce their methane emissions by 40% by 2030. To meet this goal farmers must reduce their emissions drastically. This has been a big challenge for farmers since no low-cost technology method to reduce methane has existed.



    Segmentation

    Today 10% of the methane gas emissions come from manure management and 26% from enteric fermentation from the agriculture sector in the U.S. The California Department of Food and Agriculture has invested $70M in grants to reduce methane from manure. The industry is serious in the attempts of reducing methane and great progress has been made to reduce emissions from manure.

    Enteric fermentation from cows is contributing to global warming more than manure and until now there has been no clear solution. Our goal is to solve this problem and we can do this by enabling farmers to add seaweed to cattle feed to reduce methane emissions from enteric fermentation. By making cows release less methane, the agriculture industry can take a huge leap in going sustainable.
